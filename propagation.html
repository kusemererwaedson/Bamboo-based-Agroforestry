PROPAGATION

TIGPE Bamboo Agroforestry Estates

TIGPE propagation and plantation operations is set up to produce quality planting materials through different propagation methods – sexual and asexual of different species of plants covering Bamboo, Horticultural plants, Forestry trees, Herbal Plants, Ornamental Plants and Agricultural Plants that are free from diseases.

Sexual Propagation

Sexual Propagation

We have invested in a sowing machine that sows the seed methodically in the trays. Instead of the commonly used coco peat, we use peat moss as soil; peat moss has higher water retention capacity compared to coco peat. After watering the seed is ready for germinating room.

Read More

We use the machine to fill the trays and compact it to create maximum uniformity. The machine can sow a million seedlings of cabbages per day.

After sowing, we move the seedlings to a germinating room where we maintain the right humidity and temperature. This allows the plants to germinate uniformly. Different varieties requires different conditions


From the germinating rooms, the seedlings are taken to the greenhouse, where we have built a very sophisticated and unique system that allows us to minimize on the manpower required to move plants from shade to light by changing the shading at the top.


Seedlings don’t like being moved, every movement has an adverse effect. Our irrigation systems are Italian, top-notch, computer controlled, and programmable. It’s more robot than a computer. It will do what you command it.

After 20 to 45 days, the seedlings are ready and are packed in metal racks still in the trays ready for transportation.

Asexual propagation

Tissue culture, cuttings, layering, division, budding and grafting

The Tissue Culture Laboratory is established in an area of 0.5 acre and the Greenhouse is established in 1.3 acres. The TC lab has 2 Laminar Air Flow Benches and Sub-Cultures 2,000 plants every day in 1 shift, a growing room with a total capacity to hold over 50,000 cultures at any time and supply 2 million plants every year.

At TIGPE Nurseries plant tissue culture technology is available for different plant species – bamboo, teak, potato, eucalyptus, coffee, herbs, and more. Over 10 personnel are working for the company who have been well trained in biotechnology. The lab has its own stand-by power for emergency. The technology of propagation is developed in-House for all plant species


We also propagate by; cuttings involving rooting a severed piece of the parent plant; layering involving rooting a part of the parent and then severing it; and budding and grafting involving joining two plant parts from different varieties.

Plantation

We believe that planting in collaboration with smallholder benefit their families and communities, and the environment too.
Growing bamboo-based agroforestry protects soil from erosion and degradation, captures carbon, and helps prevent the destruction of local, indigenous forests. By turning underutilized land into a network of thriving farmlands and microforests, we can make a massive environmental impact, as well as a profound impact on the lives of the people whose lives are rooted in the land
We are currently doing trials of different bamboo-based agroforestry models at our Nursery in Wakiso Town Council, Wakiso and Pilot Plantation in Apoi, Apac. The bamboo-based agroforestry models will be developed along group farming systems for scale and improved production and productivity, enabling small holder farmers to increase their revenues and be more climate SMART and market resilient.

Here's How we do it

1. Farmer enrollment

We organize meetings with the help of area LC1 and record details of the farmers in attendance: (the location, sub location, farmer name, the ID number of the farmers, thumb print, copy of ID if available, area of the farmers’ farms, area a farmer is willing to plant). Individual farm checks by the our agroforestry agents are done to evaluate the suitability of the farmer to join the program. If eligible, the farmer is formally contracted

2. Input Distribution and Planting

TIGPE Nurseries, JV sell to farmers the seedlings, or give as its share of the Musharakah. We provide other services – land opening, etc. In the Musharakah arrangement, if farmers don’t grow, neither of us gets paid. Farmers do have to set aside land plus growing the crops. Our Field Extension Network teams provide on-farm training and support.

3. Plantation Maintenance

Our Field Extension Network teams provide on-farm training and support. They collect data and monitor the plantations – and everything is coordinated through our tech platform. Most of our field staff are farmers themselves, and are our most important brand ambassadors

4. Sustainable Harvesting

Our field team and remote management technology regularly monitors and measures the progress, so we know when crops are ready for harvesting. Our specialized team harvests, then we ship it to our centralized processing centre (CPC) for processing
